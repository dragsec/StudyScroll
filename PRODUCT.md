# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

StudyScroll is for technical learners who already reach for short-form feeds during small breaks and want that habit to produce useful learning. The initial experience focuses on software-development topics and supports guests immediately, without requiring an account.

## Product Purpose

StudyScroll trains judgment rather than passive consumption. Learners inspect three plausible explanations, classify each as Legit or Sus before seeing feedback, and build a repeatable practice habit through short sessions. Success means that a learner can identify not only which answer is wrong, but why.

## Positioning

Turn the scrolling reflex into repeated practice evaluating technical explanations in an AI-saturated world.

## Brand Personality

Technical, sharp, and encouraging. The product speaks plainly, rewards careful judgment, and feels contemporary without turning learning into spectacle.

## Anti-references

StudyScroll must not feel like a casino, an attention trap, a childish quiz game, or a generic course dashboard. It avoids punishment for mistakes, endless obligation, social humiliation, pay-to-preserve mechanics, and decorative gamification that competes with learning.

## Design Principles

1. Make the judgment loop immediately understandable.
2. Require commitment before revealing feedback.
3. Treat mistakes as useful evidence, never failure.
4. Keep sessions finite even when the feed can continue.
5. Preserve source transparency and label simulated personas clearly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No project-specific accessibility requirements were supplied. Preserve the approved visual direction while using semantic HTML, keyboard-operable controls, visible focus states, non-color state labels, generous touch targets, and reduced-motion alternatives.
